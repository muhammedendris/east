east africa countries information

East Africa is a subregion of the continent of Africa, according to the United Nations Geoscheme. It consists of 18 countries and 2 dependencies. The subregion stretches from Eritrea in the north to Mozambique in the south. Off the coast of East Africa is the Indian Ocean. Middle Africa lies to the west of East Africa, while Southern Africa is situated to the southwest. The border between East Africa and North Africa consists of the borders separating the North African country of Sudan from the East African countries of South Sudan, Ethiopia, and Eritrea. Three of East Africa’s countries, Mauritius, Madagascar, and the Seychelles, are island countries, as are the French dependencies of Mayotte and Reunion. Seven of East Africa’s countries are landlocked countries

burndi

A landlocked country in East Africa, Burundi is a low-income economy, with 80% of the population employed in the agricultural sector. Located in the Great Lakes region, Burundi is surrounded by Rwanda to the north, Tanzania to the east, the Democratic Republic of the Congo to the west, and bordered by Lake Tanganyika to the southwest. With a population of 13.2 million people (2023), 50.3% of whom are women and 41.5% young people under 15, Burundi is one of the most densely populated countries in the world, with a density ratio of 442 people per square kilometer (2020 population projection).

cameron

Cameroon is a country located in Central Africa and its capital city is Yaoundé. It is bordered by Nigeria to the west, Chad to the northeast, the Central African Republic to the east, Gabon and the Republic of Congo to the south, and Equatorial Guinea to the southwest. The country has a population of over 26 million people and its official languages are English and French. The country is divided into 10 regions, two of which are English-speaking regions.

djibouti

Djibouti,[a] officially the Republic of Djibouti,[b] is a country in the Horn of Africa, bordered by Somalia[c] to the south, Ethiopia to the southwest, Eritrea in the north, and the Red Sea and the Gulf of Aden to the east.

Eritrea

Eritrea country profile 18 April 2023 Eritrea won independence from Ethiopia in 1993 after a 30-year war, but has been plagued by repression at home and tense relations with its neighbours. Bordered by Sudan, Ethiopia and Djibouti, it occupies a strategically important area in the Horn of Africa.

Ethiopia

Ethiopia is a landlocked country in the Horn of Africa1. It has the continent’s second highest population at 109,358,444 and its surface area of 1,100,000 sq. km. is the tenth largest1. Addis Ababa is the capital and the country is bordered by Sudan to the west, Djibouti and Somalia to the east, Kenya to the south, and Eritrea to the north1. Ethiopia is the oldest independent country in Africa and one of the world's oldest2. The country comprises more than 80 ethnic groups and as many languages

Kenya

Kenya is a country in East Africa12. It is bordered by South Sudan, Ethiopia, Somalia, the Indian Ocean, Tanzania, Lake Victoria, and Uganda12. Its capital is Nairobi1. Kenya was a colony of the United Kingdom from 1920 until 1963, and since then, it has been a republic with a president, a national assembly called the Bunge, and a legal system3. The country celebrates its independence day on December 12th with parades, dancing, political speeches, and feasts3.

madagascar

Madagascar is the world's fifth largest island, located off the east coast of Africa123. Its climate is tropical along the coast, temperate inland, and arid in the south1. The island has lush rain forests, tropical dry forests, plateaus, and deserts1. Madagascar was settled by humans relatively late and was a pirate stronghold in the late 17th and early 18th centuries. It was colonized by the French in 1896 and regained independence in 19604. Madagascar is known for its unique flora and fauna, including lemurs and baobab trees, and eco-tourism is a key attraction3. The country is surrounded by the Indian Ocean and is closest to Mozambique3.

malawi

Malawi, officially the Republic of Malawi, is a landlocked country in Southeastern Africa1. It shares borders with Zambia, Tanzania, and Mozambique12. The estimated population is around 20.41 million3. The country is known for Lake Malawi (formerly Lake Nyasa), one of the largest and deepest lakes in the world4. The capital city is Lilongwe, and the major languages are English and Chichewa5.

Mauritius

Mauritius is an Indian Ocean island country, east of Madagascar1. It is officially the Republic of Mauritius and its capital and largest city is Port Louis213. It has a population of about 1.3 million people23. It is known for its diverse culture, natural beauty, and wildlife, especially the extinct dodo2. The president is Ameenah Gurib-Fakim and the prime minister is Anerood Jugnauth3.

Mozambique

Mozambique is a scenic country in southeastern Africa1. It is rich in natural resources, is biologically and culturally diverse, and has a tropical climate1. Mozambique stretches for 1,535 mi (2,470 km) along Africa's southeast coast and is nearly twice the size of California2. The country is generally a low-lying plateau broken up by 25 sizable rivers that flow into the Indian Ocean2. Mozambique's extensive coastline, fronting the Mozambique Channel, offers some of Africa’s best natural harbours1.

Rwanda

Rwanda is a landlocked country in the Great Rift Valley, where the African Great Lakes region and Central Africa converge1. It is bordered by Uganda, Tanzania, Burundi, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo1. The capital is Kigali, located in the centre of the country on the Ruganwa River2. Rwanda is known for its breathtaking scenery and is often referred to as "land of a thousand hills"2. The country's history is marred by political strife and civil war, and in 1994, Rwanda was the setting for one of the world's most horrific genocides3.

Seychelles

Seychelles is a republic comprising about 115 islands in the western Indian Ocean1. It is one of the world’s smallest countries, with lush tropical vegetation, beautiful beaches, and diverse marine life1. The capital is Victoria, located on the island of Mahe1. Seychelles has a population of 97,017 (2022 est.)2. The country's president is Wavel Ramkalawan2.

Somalia

Somalia is a country located in the Horn of Africa, with a long coastline on the Gulf of Aden and the Indian Ocean12345. It is bordered by Ethiopia, Djibouti, and Kenya234. It has an area of about 637,657 square km and a population of about 15 million12. The capital and largest city is Mogadishu125. The official languages are Somali and Arabic12. The country is mostly arid and barren, with two main rivers, the Shebelle and the Juba4.

South sudan

South Sudan gained independence from Sudan on 9 July 2011 as the outcome of a 2005 agreement that ended Africa's longest-running civil war. Made up of the 10 southern-most states of Sudan, South Sudan is one of the most diverse countries in Africa. It is home to over 60 different major ethnic groups.

Tanzania

Tanzania is an East African country situated just south of the Equator12. It is the largest country in East Africa3and has an area of 947,303 sq km4. It is bordered by the Indian Ocean and eight countries, including Kenya, Uganda, Congo, and Mozambique31. It contains three of Africa's best-known lakes: Victoria, Tanganyika, and Nyasa1. Tanzania was formed in 1964 through the union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar2. It has a population of 61.7 million and its capital is Dodoma4.

Zambia

Zambia is a landlocked country in south-central Africa1. It is surrounded by Angola, Zaire, Tanzania, Malawi, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Namibia1. The country is mostly a plateau that rises to 8,000 ft (2,434 m) in the east1. Zambia has a population of 15.9 million people2. The capital and largest city is Lusaka2. The spoken languages are English and several Bantu languages, with major dialects being Bemba (ChiBemba) 33%, Nyanja (Cinyanja) 15%, Tonga (Chitonga) 11% and Lozi 5.5%2.
